Our Story

From the looms of Kancheepuram
to the homes of Dubai

Why we started Pattu House

Dubai has one of the largest South Indian communities outside India, yet finding a genuine, hand-verified Kanjivaram here has usually meant waiting for a family trip home, or trusting a saree you have never touched.

Pattu House was started to close that gap — a Dubai-based boutique that sources every saree directly from weaving families in Kancheepuram, so brides, mothers and festival-goers here can see, touch and try before they buy.

What "authentic" means to us

Authentic is not a marketing word here — it is a checklist. Real mulberry silk. Real zari, tested for silver and gold content. A named weaver behind every piece. A Silk Mark tag you can verify.

We turn away pieces that don't meet this bar, even when they are cheaper. A Kanjivaram is meant to outlast the occasion it's bought for, and we buy accordingly.

What We Believe

Three commitments behind every sale

01

Weaver-first sourcing

We pay weaving families a fair price before a saree is ever listed, so the craft stays viable for the next generation.

02

No unnamed pieces

Ask about any saree in our collection and we can tell you which weaving family made it, and roughly how long it took.

03

Try before you commit

Because a saree photographs differently than it drapes, we favour private appointments over online-only sales.

In the Showroom

Who we serve

Brides & wedding families

From the muhurtham saree to the reception piece, we help families plan a full bridal wardrobe months ahead, with fittings scheduled around the wedding calendar.

Festival & everyday wearers

Not every Kanjivaram needs to be bridal-weight. We stock lighter soft-silk weaves for Diwali, temple visits and family functions through the year.
