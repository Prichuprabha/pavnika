Why we started Pavnika by Saranya

Dubai has one of the largest South Indian communities outside India, yet finding a genuine, hand-verified Kanjivaram here has usually meant waiting for a family trip home, or trusting a saree you have never touched.

Pavnika by Saranya was started to close that gap — a Dubai-based online boutique that sources every saree directly from weaving families in Kancheepuram, so brides, mothers and festival-goers here can see, ask about and try before they buy.

What "authentic" means to us

Authentic is not a marketing word here — it is a checklist. Real silk, correctly described by weave — Kanchipuram, soft silk, semi silk, Mysore silk, Banarasi and more, each listed as what it actually is, never dressed up.

Every saree in our catalogue carries its true design and pattern from the first message. A Kanjivaram is meant to outlast the occasion it's bought for, and we catalogue accordingly.

What We Believe

Three commitments behind every sale

01

Weaver-first sourcing

We pay weaving families fairly before a saree is ever listed, so the craft stays viable for the next generation.

02

No unnamed pieces

Every saree is catalogued under its own series and ID, with real weave and pattern — nothing generic.

03

Ask before you commit

Because a saree photographs differently than it drapes, we favour a personal WhatsApp conversation over an anonymous online cart.

Word of Mouth

What our customers say

We travel to Kancheepuram ourselves to sit with the weavers, and every saree in our collection is handpicked in person — nothing chosen from a catalogue we haven't seen. Beyond what's already woven, a number of our pieces are commissioned specially: a colour, a border or a motif we've requested, brought to life on the loom exclusively for Pavnika by Saranya.

“We didn't want to be another saree shop. We wanted to be the reason a weaving family in Kancheepuram could plan their year around Dubai's wedding season.”

— Pavnika by Saranya, on our sourcing philosophy
